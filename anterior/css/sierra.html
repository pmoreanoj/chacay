LA SIERRA, THE MIGHTY ANDES

The Ecuadorian Andes region, commonly known as "La Sierra", includes ten provinces, each with its own distinct flavor and culture diversity. The Andean Highlands cover only 20% of the surface of Ecuador, but are home to nearly 50% of the Ecuador population, most of them in the UNESCO cultural heritage sites of Quito, Luz de America and the country's capital, and Cuenca, both striking colonial cities.

The Ecuadorian Andes are known for their majestic snow-capped mountains and active volcanoes and hot springs. This region of Ecuador is the most visited in the country due to the cultural and natural diversity that includes active volcanoes, green grassy paramos, humid cloud forests and fertile valleys.

Chacay is located in Quito, which makes it easy to reach most of the top destinations in the Sierra in a half-day. Because of this fact, we can plan a delightful grouping of day-tours, allowing you to return for mesmerizing evenings in Quito, the most bio diverse metropolitan capital in the world!


Excursions include Papallacta hot springs, where you can spot a condor while soaking in natural sulfur baths; Banyos, the country's resort village at the foot of the active volcano Tunguragua, where you can try out canyoning (rapelling down rapids), biking, rafting, trekking, ziplining, and bungee jumping; Riobamba, Ecuador's first capital and origin of the breathtaking, world renowned Nariz del Diablo (Nose of the Devil) train ride, an engineering feat in its day (early 19th century); Mindo, a bird watching paradise nestled in the Cloud Forest with a vast array of species including humming birds, toucans, the brilliant cock-of-the-rock, as well as orchids and butterflies; Quilotoa and Salinas de Guaranda, spectacular and colorful indigenous communities with breathtaking handicrafts at extraordinary prices; and Otavalo, Cotacachi and San Antonio de Ibarra, Latin America's top destinations for textiles, leather goods, and wood carvings.
