Ecuador's Amazon Rain Forest

Chacay has established partnerships with a variety of lodges dedicated to ecotourism, environmental conservation, and community service in the Amazon rainforest of Ecuador. Most of the lodges are owned and operated by members of the local communities, meaning that with their extensive knowledge of the Amazon wildlife and biodiversity they can give Chacay guests a unique and genuine introduction to the Ecuadorian Amazon jungle. Chacay`s lodges are built to offer you comfort in cabins with essential services, emphasizing quality rather than quantity, offering a more intimate experience with the indigenous people and the flora and fauna of the rainforest.


Itineraries:

Our itineraries, come with activities of 4 or 5 days: the following is a sample of one of our 4 days – 3 nights itineraries.

Location: lower part of Cuyabeno Reserve (Aguarico and Cuyabeno Rivers)


Day 1.- After a morning flight from Quito, you'll travel to Tierras Orientales where you will start your 3 hour canoe journey down the Aguarico and continue along the Cuyabeno Black Water River. The ride takes you to the front steps of your Lodge where you will be welcomed by members of the Siona Tribe. You'll have a chance to catch your breath and explore the compound. After dinner, your naturalist (and bilingual!) guide will introduce you to wonders of the Amazon Rain Forest, focusing in particular on the Cuyabeno Natural Reserve and the culture of the Siona.

Day 2.- You will start the day with an early morning canoe excursion, as the brisk morning hours are the best time to experience the thriving rainforest. After breakfast, we trek in primary rainforest with our Naturalist and native guides who will explain the practical and medicinal uses of various trees and plants. After lunch, another excursion paddling or by motorized canoes to enjoy the beautiful landscapes and to try to spot species of monkeys or exquisite pink fresh water dolphins. After dinner we will venture for a night stroll through the forest to find species of insects, and with a bit of luck, species of snakes or frogs.

Day 3.- Again, we set out early in the morning and spend the day exploring. We navigate downstream to observe the enchanting salt lick (exposed clay bank) where colorful Parrots and Parakeets feed on the clay to draw the toxins from their bodies. Return to the Lodge and after breakfast we trek into pristine primary rainforest. Our native guide shares his wealth of knowledge, showing us the medicinal and practical uses of the rainforest flora and fauna that their ancestors have passed down through the generations, and we learn how all life in the tropical rainforest co-exists in a fragile balance. After lunch, we navigate along the Cuyabeno River for another opportunity of seeing the freshwater pink and grey river dolphins (Inia geoffrensis and Sotalia fluviatilis) in the spectacular scenery. We also try our luck at Piranha fishing. We climb the Kichwa Birdwatching Tower; here above the canopy we have a breathtaking view of the surrounding rainforest, and can observe a variety of species including Toucans, Parrots, Monkeys, and many more. After dinner, we venture out on a night excursion to search for Caimans (alligator family) and nocturnal birds.

Day 4.- After breakfast, we navigate along Cuyabeno rivers to the Kichwa Indian Community where our naturalist guide explains the culture and traditions of the native people from the past to the present. We travel by canoe down the Cuyabeno River and back along the Aguarico River to Pto. Gregorio, where our bus/truck will take us to Lago Agrio. Departure by plane or bus for the return trip to Quito.
